Malicious Attachments Can Contain Ransomware

Cyber criminals often use attachments in phishing emails that are actually “malware” (i.e., malicious software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to your computer). With a single click, you can inadvertently fall victim to a ransomware cyber-attack. It can happen to anyone. Luckily, this one was just a drill.

When dangerous phishing emails make it through our cybersecurity defenses, we rely on you as an important line of defense. These phishing attacks are easier to spot once you know what to look for. Be on the lookout for the following indicators that might help point out a malicious email attachment.

 

  • Unusual or oddly titled files: While some file extensions are more obviously malicious (e.g., .exe, .vbs, .wsf), cyber criminals can use .pdf, .doc, .xls, and .zip files as malicious attachments containing ransomware too. Check file names to make sure you know what file type you are opening. For example, a cyber criminal may title a file “teamphoto.jpg.exe” to trick you into thinking you are opening an image.
  • Unusual and urgent request: If a client, vendor, or partner asks you to open a file urgently or within an unrealistic amount of time, pause before opening and confirm with the sender that it is legitimate.
  • Unexpected or odd message: If you receive an attachment from a colleague or friend that you were not expecting, their account could be compromised. If the message body is odd (i.e., it contains vague information) and includes an attachment, confirm with the sender that the attachment and email are legitimate
  • Unfamiliar or external sender: You will see a [EXT] flag in the subject field of emails that come from outside Deloitte. You should use caution if you are not familiar with the sender. Never click on an attachment from an unknown sender. Always confirm that the sender is legitimate before opening any attachments.

 

In the future, if you receive a suspicious email, report it by clicking the “Report Phishing/ Report Suspicious Email” button in your Outlook ribbon. Thank you for staying vigilant
